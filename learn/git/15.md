## What Have I Learned?

I learned that `git stash` shelves uncommitted, unstaged, staged work and clean the working tree.

`git stash list` shows shelf entries, and where your branch was when you stashed.

`git stash show -p` peeks at what is inside. `-p` stands for `patch`, it shows the line-by-line changes.

You can switch branches freely and safely while WIP (Work in progress) is stashed.

`git stash pop` restores and removes it from shelf.

`git stash apply` restores but also still keeps on shelf, you can reuse it later, maybe on another branch.
Then you should `git stash drop` to remove it from shelf or you can use `git stash pop` at your last use.
