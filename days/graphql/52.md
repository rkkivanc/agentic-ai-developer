# Day 52: Subscriptions & Realtime - WebSockets, SSE, and Transport Options

## 📝 Today's Tasks

1.  **Compare Transports:** Contrast WebSockets, SSE, and multipart subscriptions for your stack.
2.  **Configure Yoga or Apollo:** Enable the subscription server alongside your HTTP listener.

## 📖 Task Explanations

Subscriptions need a persistent channel. WebSockets are most common; SSE and HTTP/2 streams appear in newer setups. Apollo Server and GraphQL Yoga document supported transports for your deployment target.

Production requires thinking about proxies, load balancers, and sticky sessions—sketch how your chosen transport behaves behind TLS terminators.

## 💡 What You'll Gain

By the end of today, you will understand subscription transport options and configure one locally.

## 📚 Dictionary for Developers

*   **WebSocket:** Bidirectional protocol often used for GraphQL subscriptions.
*   **SSE (Server-Sent Events):** One-way HTTP streaming from server to client.
*   **Subscription Transport:** The underlying protocol carrying subscription events.
*   **Sticky Session:** Routing the same client to the same server instance for open sockets.
*   **Connection Init:** Handshake payload often carrying auth tokens for subscriptions.
