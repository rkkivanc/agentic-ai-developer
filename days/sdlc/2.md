# Day 2: SDLC Fundamentals - Classic Phases End to End

## 📝 Today's Tasks

1.  **Phase Walkthrough:** For each major phase, write one input and one output.
2.  **Handoffs:** Describe what 'done' means moving from design to implementation and from testing to release.
3.  **Roles Snapshot:** Match roles (PM/PO, designer, developer, QA, DevOps) to phases they heavily influence.
4.  **Mini Timeline:** Sketch a 6-box timeline for a small feature from request to production.

## 📖 Task Explanations

Phases only help when handoffs are clear: what enters a phase and what leaves it. Roles overlap, but each phase has owners and quality bars. A simple timeline makes the cycle concrete for a single feature.

## 💡 What You'll Gain

You will describe each SDLC phase with inputs/outputs and sketch a feature's journey from request to production.

## 📚 Dictionary for Developers

*   **Handoff:** Passing work and context from one phase or role to another.
*   **Definition of Done:** Agreed criteria that make a piece of work complete.
*   **Product Owner / PM:** Role focused on priorities, requirements, and stakeholder value.
*   **QA:** Quality assurance—practices and people focused on verifying software quality.
