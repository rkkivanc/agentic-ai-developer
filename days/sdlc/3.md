# Day 3: SDLC Fundamentals - Waterfall, Agile, and Iterative Models

## 📝 Today's Tasks

1.  **Waterfall Sketch:** Draw a linear sequence of phases; list 2 strengths and 2 weaknesses.
2.  **Agile Sketch:** Describe short iterations, feedback, and changing priorities.
3.  **Compare for a Project:** Choose a sample project (bank core vs marketing landing page) and pick a better model.
4.  **Hybrid Reality:** Note how many real teams mix planning discipline with iterative delivery.

## 📖 Task Explanations

Waterfall emphasizes upfront planning and sequential phases. Agile emphasizes working software in short cycles with frequent feedback. Most organizations use hybrids. Matching the model to risk, regulation, and change rate is a professional judgment skill.

## 💡 What You'll Gain

You will contrast Waterfall and Agile/iterative approaches and justify a model choice for a sample project.

## 📚 Dictionary for Developers

*   **Waterfall:** A sequential SDLC model where phases largely complete before the next begins.
*   **Agile:** An iterative approach valuing working software, collaboration, and response to change.
*   **Iteration / Sprint:** A short timeboxed cycle that delivers a usable increment.
*   **Hybrid Model:** A blend of predictive planning and iterative delivery.
