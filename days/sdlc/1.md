# Day 1: SDLC Fundamentals - What Is the Software Development Life Cycle

## 📝 Today's Tasks

1.  **Define SDLC:** Write your own definition of the software development life cycle in 3–5 sentences.
2.  **List Core Phases:** Identify common phases (requirements, design, implementation, testing, deployment, maintenance).
3.  **Map a Familiar App:** Pick an app you use and guess which phase produced a recent change you noticed.
4.  **Why It Matters:** Note 3 risks of skipping a phase (e.g., building without requirements).

## 📖 Task Explanations

The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) is the structured path from idea to running software and ongoing care. Teams use phases and checkpoints so work stays visible, testable, and improvable. Today you learn the big picture before diving into models and practices.

## 💡 What You'll Gain

By the end of today, you will explain what SDLC means, list its main phases, and connect them to real software you already use.

## 📚 Dictionary for Developers

*   **SDLC:** Software Development Life Cycle—the end-to-end process of planning, building, shipping, and maintaining software.
*   **Phase:** A major stage of the life cycle with its own goals and outputs.
*   **Deliverable:** A concrete artifact produced by a phase (e.g., requirements doc, design, release).
*   **Stakeholder:** Anyone affected by or interested in the software (users, clients, ops, business).
