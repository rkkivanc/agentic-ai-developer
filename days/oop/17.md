# Day 17: SOLID, Patterns & Practice - LSP, ISP, and DIP

## 📝 Today's Tasks

1.  **LSP Check:** Ensure subclasses can replace bases without surprising callers.
2.  **ISP Split:** Break a fat interface into smaller, focused contracts.
3.  **DIP Wire:** Make a high-level policy depend on an abstraction; inject the concrete type.
4.  **Violation Hunt:** Find or invent one violation for each principle and fix it.

## 📖 Task Explanations

Liskov Substitution keeps inheritance honest. Interface Segregation avoids forcing unused methods. Dependency Inversion makes systems testable by depending on abstractions. Together with SRP/OCP, these form SOLID.

## 💡 What You'll Gain

You will recognize and fix LSP, ISP, and DIP issues in small designs.

## 📚 Dictionary for Developers

*   **LSP:** Subtypes must be substitutable for their base types.
*   **ISP:** Prefer small, client-specific interfaces over fat ones.
*   **DIP:** Depend on abstractions, not concretions.
*   **Fat Interface:** An interface with too many unrelated methods.
