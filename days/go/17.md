# Day 17: Concurrency Basics - Channels

## 📝 Today's Tasks

1.  **Create Channels:** Use `make(chan T)` for unbuffered channels and experiment with sends and receives.
2.  **Buffered Channels:** Use buffer sizes to decouple producers and consumers modestly.
3.  **Close and Range:** Close channels when done and use `for range` to drain values.
4.  **Coordinate Workers:** Build a simple worker pattern passing jobs and results on channels.

## 📖 Task Explanations

Channels are Go's typed conduits for communication between goroutines. The mantra is: share memory by communicating, not the other way around.

You'll practice synchronizing with unbuffered channels and smoothing bursts with buffers. Closing channels signals completion; ranging over a channel is a clean consumer pattern.

A tiny worker pool preview shows how channels orchestrate real systems you'll build later.

## 💡 What You'll Gain

By the end of today, you will use channels to pass data safely between goroutines and coordinate simple worker flows.

## 📚 Dictionary for Developers

*   **Channel:** A typed conduit for sending and receiving values between goroutines.
*   **Unbuffered Channel:** A channel that synchronizes sender and receiver at the handoff point.
*   **Buffered Channel:** A channel with capacity that accepts sends without an immediate receiver.
*   **Close:** Signaling no more values will be sent; receivers still drain remaining values.
*   **Worker Pattern:** A design where goroutines process jobs delivered through channels.
