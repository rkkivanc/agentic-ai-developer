# Day 49: Databases (II) & Repositories - Connection Pooling

## 📝 Today's Tasks

1.  **Tune Pool Settings:** Adjust `SetMaxOpenConns`, `SetMaxIdleConns`, and connection lifetime.
2.  **Watch Saturation:** Log or metric when waits for connections increase.
3.  **Close on Shutdown:** Close the DB pool during graceful shutdown.
4.  **Read Driver Notes:** Review driver-specific pooling recommendations.

## 📖 Task Explanations

`database/sql` pools connections automatically, but defaults are not always right. Too few connections starve handlers; too many overwhelm the database.

Tune pool sizes for your workload and environment. Close the pool on shutdown to avoid hanging deploys.

Connection waits often show up as latency spikes before obvious errors—watch early.

## 💡 What You'll Gain

By the end of today, you will tune database connection pooling and close resources on shutdown.

## 📚 Dictionary for Developers

*   **MaxOpenConns:** Limit on open connections to the database from this process.
*   **MaxIdleConns:** Connections kept idle in the pool for reuse.
*   **Pool Saturation:** When all connections are busy and new queries must wait.
*   **Connection Lifetime:** Maximum age of a connection before it is retired from the pool.
*   **Latency Spike:** Sudden increase in response time, sometimes caused by pool exhaustion.
