## 📖 Task Explanations

Today marks the completion of your 100-day Go journey! You'll add advanced CI/CD touches where practical, then review the capstone holistically.

Advanced CI/CD features include:
- **Matrix Builds:** Testing multiple Go versions or platforms simultaneously
- **Conditional Steps:** Running jobs only on tags or main branch
- **Artifacts:** Saving binaries or coverage reports for download
- **Notifications:** Alerting when pipelines fail

For your final review, ensure the capstone demonstrates HTTP and/or gRPC APIs, persistence, auth, tests, observability, containers, and CI. Update portfolio README to tell the story clearly.

Finally, reflect on your journey—from Go fundamentals to professional delivery. Note what to deepen next: Kubernetes, advanced distributed systems, or domain-specific platforms.

## 💡 What You'll Gain

By the end of today, you will have completed your 100-day Go journey with a capstone, advanced delivery workflow, and a clear plan for continued growth.

## 📚 Dictionary for Developers

*   **Matrix Builds:** CI jobs running the same steps across multiple configurations in parallel.
*   **Build Artifacts:** Outputs such as binaries, images, or reports stored from CI runs.
*   **Portfolio README:** A summary document highlighting projects and skills for employers or clients.
*   **Professional Workflow:** Practices including tests, reviews, CI/CD, docs, and operability.
*   **Continuous Learning:** Ongoing skill development expected throughout a Go engineering career.
