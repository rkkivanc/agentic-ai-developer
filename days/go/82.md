# Day 82: Caching & Messaging - Message Queues Overview

## 📝 Today's Tasks

1.  **Learn Queue Concepts:** Understand producers, consumers, topics, and acknowledgments.
2.  **Pick a Broker:** Explore NATS, RabbitMQ, or Kafka concepts at a high level.

## 📖 Task Explanations

Message queues decouple producers and consumers in time. They smooth spikes and move slow work off request paths.

Brokers differ in durability and complexity—start conceptually, then pick one tool for exercises. Async tasks like emails belong off the critical HTTP path.

Consumers must tolerate duplicates—at-least-once delivery is common.

## 💡 What You'll Gain

By the end of today, you will understand queue concepts and identify async work suitable for messaging.

## 📚 Dictionary for Developers

*   **Message Queue:** A broker buffering messages between producers and consumers.
*   **Producer:** A component publishing messages to a queue or topic.
*   **Consumer:** A component processing messages from a queue.
*   **Acknowledgment:** A signal telling the broker a message was processed successfully.
*   **At-Least-Once Delivery:** Messages may arrive more than once; consumers must handle duplicates.
